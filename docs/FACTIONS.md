# Factions of the Ruined City

## Overview
Eight major factions compete for control of the city's ruins. Each has distinct starting positions, cultural tendencies, personalities, and strategic goals. While player factions develop emergent culture through choices, AI factions have cultural preferences that guide their decisions.

---

## 1. The Vault Collective

### Identity
*"Preserving yesterday, protecting tomorrow"*

Survivors who sheltered in a pre-war corporate vault complex during the collapse. They emerged with preserved technology, knowledge, and a mission to restore the old world's glory.

### Starting Location
**Downtown Financial District** - Underground vault beneath former corporate headquarters

### Starting Resources
- **Unique Locations Controlled**:
  - Corporate Vault (secure housing, +20% security, population growth)
  - Data Archive (pre-war records, +5 research/turn)
  - Executive Bunker (fortified command center)

- **Starting Stockpile**:
  - Scrap: 200
  - Food: 150 (preserved rations)
  - Medicine: 100
  - Components: 50 (pre-war electronics)
  - Water: 200 (purification system)

### Cultural Tendencies
- **Governance**: Democratic (Council of Vault Directors)
- **Belief**: Secular → Rationalist
- **Technology**: Preservationist (strong preference)
- **Social**: Meritocratic

### Faction Bonuses
- **"Vault-Tec Legacy"**: +10% research from pre-war facilities
- **"We Prepared for This"**: Immune to first starvation/dehydration event
- **"Bunker Mentality"**: +15% defense when fighting underground

### Personality & Diplomacy
- **Traits**: Cautious, Intellectual, Preservationist
- **Likes**: Knowledge sharing, peaceful expansion, trade
- **Dislikes**: Destruction of pre-war sites, raider activity, primitivism
- **Diplomatic Style**: Formal, bureaucratic, alliance-focused
- **AI Behavior**:
  - Prioritizes research facilities
  - Defensive early game, expands carefully
  - Seeks alliances with peaceful factions
  - Goes to war to protect knowledge sites

### Unique Unit
**Vault Guardian** (150 PP, 40 scrap, 25 ammo)
- HP: 90 | Attack: 22 | Defense: 14 | Range: 3 | Movement: 3
- Armor: 20% (pre-war security armor)
- Special: "Vault Training" (+5 defense when defending controlled territory)

### Victory Path Preference
Primary: **Technological Singularity**
Secondary: **The New Order** (diplomatic)

### Leader
**Director Helena Voss** - Former executive turned vault leader. Pragmatic, intelligent, believes humanity can rebuild properly with the right knowledge and planning.

### Story Hook
The Vault Collective's archives contain fragments about what caused the collapse. They're pursuing this mystery while trying to prevent it from happening again.

## 2. The Rust Brothers

### Identity
*"Engines are prayer, machines are salvation"*

Mechanist cult who worship pre-war industrial machinery and internal combustion engines. Skilled engineers, traders, and the city's best hope for vehicle warfare.

### Starting Location
**Industrial District** - Old automobile factory complex

### Starting Resources
- **Unique Locations Controlled**:
  - Auto Factory (can produce motorcycles)
  - Machine Shop (+15 PP/turn, can build vehicles)
  - Fuel Depot (starting 300 fuel)

- **Starting Stockpile**:
  - Scrap: 400 (highest starting scrap)
  - Food: 80
  - Medicine: 40
  - Components: 30
  - Fuel: 300
  - Water: 120

### Cultural Tendencies
- **Governance**: Collectivist (Workers' Brotherhood)
- **Belief**: Materialist → Mystic Order (machine worship)
- **Technology**: Innovationist (improvised engineering)
- **Social**: Meritocratic (best engineers lead)

### Faction Bonuses
- **"Grease Monkeys"**: Vehicles cost -25% and consume -1 fuel/turn
- **"Scrap Kings"**: +3 scrap per turn from scavenging
- **"Jury-Rigged"**: Can repair destroyed buildings to 50% functionality

### Personality & Diplomacy
- **Traits**: Industrious, Pragmatic, Greedy
- **Likes**: Trade (especially components and fuel), industrial sites
- **Dislikes**: Destruction of machinery, waste, primitivists
- **Diplomatic Style**: Merchant-focused, transactional, friendly but self-interested
- **AI Behavior**:
  - Establishes many trade routes
  - Prioritizes industrial and fuel locations
  - Builds vehicles early
  - Raids for resources but prefers trade

### Unique Unit
**Motorized Raider** (250 PP, 60 scrap, 40 components, 30 fuel)
- Motorcycle unit with extra armor
- HP: 100 | Attack: 20 | Defense: 12 | Range: 2 | Movement: 12
- Special: "Hit and Run" (can attack and disengage without retaliation)

### Victory Path Preference
Primary: **The New Order** (economic/diplomatic)
Secondary: **Territorial Supremacy** (via vehicle dominance)

### Leader
**Chief Engineer Marcus "Wrench" Torque** - Charismatic mechanic who found religion in a perfectly preserved 1967 Mustang. Believes engines are humanity's divine creation.

### Story Hook
The Rust Brothers are searching for the legendary "Tank Factory" rumored to exist somewhere in the military district. Finding it would make them dominant.

## 3. The Green Faith

### Identity
*"The concrete jungle dies, the real jungle lives"*

Eco-mystics who believe the collapse was nature's judgment on humanity's hubris. They're reclaiming the city for the natural world while building a sustainable, agrarian society.

### Starting Location
**Central Park & Botanical Gardens** - Nature has reclaimed this area

### Starting Resources
- **Unique Locations Controlled**:
  - Botanical Gardens (vertical farm, +40 food/turn)
  - Nature Reserve (clean water, hunting grounds)
  - Greenhouse Complex (+30 food/turn)

- **Starting Stockpile**:
  - Scrap: 50 (reject technology)
  - Food: 300 (highest starting food)
  - Medicine: 60 (herbal)
  - Components: 0
  - Water: 250 (natural springs)

### Cultural Tendencies
- **Governance**: Tribal → Clan Federation
- **Belief**: Spiritual → Mystic Order
- **Technology**: Primitivist (strong preference)
- **Social**: Egalitarian

### Faction Bonuses
- **"Children of the Earth"**: +50% food production, +2 happiness in natural terrain
- **"Rewilding"**: Controlled tiles slowly remove radiation (5% per 5 turns)
- **"Nature's Allies"**: Can recruit feral creatures as combat units

### Personality & Diplomacy
- **Traits**: Peaceful (unless provoked), Isolationist, Environmentalist
- **Likes**: Agriculture, nature preservation, primitivist cultures
- **Dislikes**: Pollution, heavy industry, war, technology abuse
- **Diplomatic Style**: Spiritual, idealistic, reluctant to engage
- **AI Behavior**:
  - Highly defensive, rarely attacks first
  - Prioritizes parks, gardens, waterfront
  - Trades food for peace
  - Will fight fiercely if farmlands threatened

### Unique Unit
**Guerrilla Ranger** (80 PP, 15 scrap)
- Nature warrior with survival skills
- HP: 65 | Attack: 18 | Defense: 10 | Range: 3 | Movement: 5
- Stealth: +4 in natural terrain
- Special: "Ambush" (+50% attack on first strike from stealth)

### Victory Path Preference
Primary: **Cultural Ascendancy** (peaceful conversion)
Secondary: **Territorial Supremacy** (defensive expansion)

### Leader
**Elder Sage Willow Chen** - Former botanist who survived by taking shelter in the botanical gardens' underground seed vault. Now sees the collapse as spiritual opportunity.

### Story Hook
The Green Faith possesses secret knowledge about radiation-resistant crops that could feed the entire city. Others covet this knowledge.

## 4. The Corporate Remnant

### Identity
*"Profit above all, efficiency eternal"*

Survivors of a mega-corporation's private security and management. They run their faction like a business: efficient, ruthless, profit-driven, and surprisingly effective.

### Starting Location
**Corporate Campus** - Fortified office park with private security

### Starting Resources
- **Unique Locations Controlled**:
  - Corporate HQ (command & control)
  - Private Security Armory (+15 ammo/turn)
  - Employee Housing (dense population)

- **Starting Stockpile**:
  - Scrap: 150
  - Food: 120
  - Medicine: 80
  - Ammunition: 200 (private security stockpile)
  - Water: 150

### Cultural Tendencies
- **Governance**: Autocratic (CEO rules absolutely)
- **Belief**: Materialist → Corporate State
- **Technology**: Preservationist or Innovationist (whatever's profitable)
- **Social**: Hierarchical (strong preference)

### Faction Bonuses
- **"Synergy Dividends"**: +20% production from all sources
- **"Hostile Takeover"**: Can vassalize defeated factions as "subsidiaries"
- **"Quarterly Returns"**: Every 20 turns, gain bonus resources based on controlled sites

### Personality & Diplomacy
- **Traits**: Efficient, Ruthless, Mercantile
- **Likes**: Trade, profit, efficiency, hierarchical order
- **Dislikes**: Inefficiency, egalitarianism, charity
- **Diplomatic Style**: Business negotiations, contracts, conditional agreements
- **AI Behavior**:
  - Aggressive expansion early
  - Cost-benefit analysis for war
  - Trades extensively
  - Will betray allies if profitable

### Unique Unit
**Executive Enforcer** (200 PP, 50 scrap, 30 ammo, 15 components)
- Corporate security with advanced equipment
- HP: 85 | Attack: 24 | Defense: 12 | Range: 3 | Movement: 4
- Armor: 15%
- Special: "Corporate Efficiency" (costs -25% maintenance)

### Victory Path Preference
Primary: **The New Order** (economic takeover)
Secondary: **Territorial Supremacy** (corporate conquest)

### Leader
**CEO Drake Mercer** - Former VP of Security who took control during the collapse. Views the post-apocalypse as the ultimate market opportunity.

### Story Hook
The Corporate Remnant has access to pre-war financial records showing where valuable stockpiles were hidden. They're methodically claiming these "assets."

## 5. The Free City

### Identity
*"Democracy didn't die, it evolved"*

Coalition of civilian survivors who banded together for mutual protection. They're trying to rebuild democratic governance, civil society, and hope itself.

### Starting Location
**Old Town** - Historic district with town hall

### Starting Resources
- **Unique Locations Controlled**:
  - City Hall (governance center, +2 culture/turn)
  - Community Center (social hub, +1 happiness)
  - Public Library (+3 research/turn)

- **Starting Stockpile**:
  - Scrap: 120
  - Food: 180
  - Medicine: 100
  - Ammunition: 80
  - Water: 200

### Cultural Tendencies
- **Governance**: Democratic (strong preference)
- **Belief**: Secular or Spiritual (varies)
- **Technology**: Balanced (no strong preference)
- **Social**: Egalitarian (strong preference)

### Faction Bonuses
- **"Unity in Diversity"**: +3 happiness baseline, reduced unrest
- **"Democratic Mandate"**: Can annex willing populations peacefully
- **"Volunteer Army"**: Militia units cost -50% but 10% weaker

### Personality & Diplomacy
- **Traits**: Idealistic, Diplomatic, Defensive
- **Likes**: Democracy, egalitarianism, peace, culture
- **Dislikes**: Autocracy, raiders, oppression
- **Diplomatic Style**: Principled, alliance-seeking, humanitarian
- **AI Behavior**:
  - Forms alliances readily
  - Defensive military stance
  - Cultural expansion focus
  - Will fight to defend allies

### Unique Unit
**People's Defender** (100 PP, 20 scrap, 15 ammo)
- Volunteer militia with high morale
- HP: 70 | Attack: 16 | Defense: 10 | Range: 2 | Movement: 4
- Morale: Starts at 90 (vs normal 70)
- Special: "For Freedom!" (+20% attack when defending Free City territory)

### Victory Path Preference
Primary: **Cultural Ascendancy**
Secondary: **The New Order** (democratic unification)

### Leader
**Mayor Sarah Chen** - Former city councilwoman who organized survivors into a functioning government. Believes civilization requires consent of the governed.

### Story Hook
The Free City is trying to hold a city-wide election to establish legitimate government. Other factions must decide whether to participate or reject the process.

## 6. The Crimson Horde

### Identity
*"The strong take, the weak serve"*

Raider confederation united under a brutal but charismatic warlord. They believe the apocalypse revealed truth: only strength matters.

### Starting Location
**Highway Interchange & Stadium** - Fortified raider compound

### Starting Resources
- **Unique Locations Controlled**:
  - Raider Fortress (fortified stadium, +20% military production)
  - Highway System (fast movement, raid staging)
  - Fight Pits (trains warriors, +1 XP to new units)

- **Starting Stockpile**:
  - Scrap: 250 (looted)
  - Food: 60 (raiding for food)
  - Medicine: 30
  - Ammunition: 180
  - Fuel: 100 (motorcycles)
  - Water: 80

### Cultural Tendencies
- **Governance**: Autocratic → Warlord State
- **Belief**: Nihilist → Darwinist Empire
- **Technology**: Primitivist or Innovationist (improvised weapons)
- **Social**: Hierarchical

### Faction Bonuses
- **"Live by the Sword"**: +25% loot from all sources, +15% attack
- **"Fear the Horde"**: Enemy adjacent territories suffer -2 happiness
- **"Raider Culture"**: Can vassalize defeated factions as tributaries

### Personality & Diplomacy
- **Traits**: Aggressive, Opportunistic, Brutal
- **Likes**: Strength, tribute, conquest
- **Dislikes**: Weakness, pacifism, democracy
- **Diplomatic Style**: Demands, threats, temporary truces
- **AI Behavior**:
  - Constant aggression and raiding
  - Opportunistic attacks on weak factions
  - Takes tribute instead of trading
  - Will ally if facing stronger enemy

### Unique Unit
**Crimson Berserker** (120 PP, 30 scrap, 20 ammo)
- Fanatical warrior with no fear
- HP: 80 | Attack: 28 | Defense: 6 | Range: 1 | Movement: 5
- Morale: Immune to morale loss
- Special: "Blood Frenzy" (+50% attack when charging, cannot retreat)

### Victory Path Preference
Primary: **Raider King** (rule through fear)
Secondary: **Territorial Supremacy** (conquest)

### Leader
**Warlord Kane "The Reaper"** - Former MMA fighter who became raider king through brutal combat trials. Believes compassion died with the old world.

### Story Hook
The Crimson Horde controls the city's only intact fuel refinery. They can choke off vehicle warfare for everyone else, giving them massive leverage.

## 7. The Old Guard

### Identity
*"We swore an oath, we keep our oath"*

Remnants of military and police trying to restore order and law. They're overwhelmed but disciplined, attempting to protect civilians and rebuild government authority.

### Starting Location
**Military Base & Precinct** - Fortified military compound

### Starting Resources
- **Unique Locations Controlled**:
  - Military Base (training grounds, +2 XP to new units)
  - Armory (+25 ammo/turn)
  - National Guard Depot (military equipment)

- **Starting Stockpile**:
  - Scrap: 100
  - Food: 140
  - Medicine: 120
  - Ammunition: 400 (highest starting ammunition)
  - Components: 40
  - Water: 160

### Cultural Tendencies
- **Governance**: Autocratic → Military Dictatorship OR Democratic (internal conflict)
- **Belief**: Secular → Rationalist
- **Technology**: Preservationist
- **Social**: Meritocratic or Hierarchical

### Faction Bonuses
- **"Military Training"**: All units start with +1 XP level (Veteran)
- **"Fortified Positions"**: All controlled territories get +5 defense
- **"Chain of Command"**: +20% production for military units

### Personality & Diplomacy
- **Traits**: Honorable, Disciplined, Protective
- **Likes**: Order, law, protecting civilians, fellow military
- **Dislikes**: Raiders, chaos, lawlessness
- **Diplomatic Style**: Military protocols, formal, alliance-focused
- **AI Behavior**:
  - Defensive perimeter expansion
  - Protects civilian factions from raiders
  - Highly organized military strategy
  - Seeks to establish martial law

### Unique Unit
**Veteran Soldier** (180 PP, 35 scrap, 30 ammo)
- Professional military with pre-war training
- HP: 85 | Attack: 23 | Defense: 13 | Range: 3 | Movement: 4
- Starts at Veteran rank (+10% attack/defense)
- Special: "Suppressive Fire" (can pin down enemy units, -2 movement)

### Victory Path Preference
Primary: **The New Order** (military-backed government)
Secondary: **Territorial Supremacy** (pacification)

### Leader
**General Marcus Stone** - Career officer who was off-duty during the collapse. Wracked with guilt, he's trying to save what remains of civilization through military order.

### Story Hook
The Old Guard has access to a military satellite communication system. If they can restore it, they might contact other survivor groups beyond the city - or discover there are none.

## 8. The Children of Atom

### Identity
*"Embrace Division, for Division is the glow that brings life"*

Radiation-worshipping cult who believe the apocalypse was divine judgment and cleansing. They see radiation as sacred and the old world's destruction as necessary.

### Starting Location
**Nuclear Power Plant** - Irradiated zone, deadly to most

### Starting Resources
- **Unique Locations Controlled**:
  - Nuclear Power Plant (dangerous but valuable)
  - Irradiated Temple (cultist gathering site)
  - Glowing Caves (radiation-rich, sacred site)

- **Starting Stockpile**:
  - Scrap: 80
  - Food: 100
  - Medicine: 50
  - Ammunition: 60
  - Water: 120 (don't need clean water)
  - Components: 20

### Cultural Tendencies
- **Governance**: Tribal or Autocratic (prophet rules)
- **Belief**: Spiritual → Mystic Order (radiation worship)
- **Technology**: Primitivist OR Transhumanist (radiation adaptation)
- **Social**: Hierarchical (priests above laypeople)

### Faction Bonuses
- **"Embrace the Glow"**: Immune to radiation damage, units heal in radiation
- **"Nuclear Blessing"**: Can create radiation zones (area denial weapon)
- **"Glowing Ones"**: Can recruit irradiated creatures as units

### Personality & Diplomacy
- **Traits**: Fanatical, Isolationist, Unpredictable
- **Likes**: Radiation, nuclear sites, converts to their faith
- **Dislikes**: Radiation cleanup, technology (except nuclear), outsiders
- **Diplomatic Style**: Prophetic, cryptic, conversion-focused
- **AI Behavior**:
  - Controls irradiated zones others can't access
  - Spreads radiation (terraforming for them)
  - Proselytizes and converts captured populations
  - Unpredictable aggression

### Unique Unit
**Glowing Zealot** (90 PP, 20 scrap)
- Irradiated cultist warrior
- HP: 70 | Attack: 20 | Defense: 8 | Range: 1 | Movement: 4
- Immune to radiation
- Special: "Radioactive Touch" (melee attacks irradiate enemies, -5 HP/turn)

### Victory Path Preference
Primary: **Cultural Ascendancy** (convert the city)
Secondary: **Territorial Supremacy** (make city uninhabitable to non-believers)

### Leader
**Prophet Confessor Cromwell II** - Charismatic preacher who survived lethal radiation exposure through mutation or sheer fanaticism. Believes he's been chosen to spread Division's word.

### Story Hook
The Children of Atom know the nuclear power plant can be restarted - providing massive energy but risking catastrophic meltdown. They're willing to do it.

## Faction Relationships Matrix

### Starting Relationships

|                  | Vault | Rust | Green | Corp | Free City | Crimson | Old Guard | Atom |
|------------------|-------|------|-------|------|-----------|---------|-----------|------|
| **Vault Collective** | - | Friendly | Neutral | Rival | Allied | Enemy | Friendly | Neutral |
| **Rust Brothers** | Friendly | - | Neutral | Friendly | Neutral | Neutral | Friendly | Neutral |
| **Green Faith** | Neutral | Neutral | - | Enemy | Friendly | Enemy | Neutral | Rival |
| **Corporate Remnant** | Rival | Friendly | Enemy | - | Rival | Neutral | Neutral | Neutral |
| **Free City** | Allied | Neutral | Friendly | Rival | - | Enemy | Allied | Rival |
| **Crimson Horde** | Enemy | Neutral | Enemy | Neutral | Enemy | - | Enemy | Neutral |
| **Old Guard** | Friendly | Friendly | Neutral | Neutral | Allied | Enemy | - | Rival |
| **Children of Atom** | Neutral | Neutral | Rival | Neutral | Rival | Neutral | Rival | - |

**Key**:
- **Allied**: Mutual defense pact, shared vision
- **Friendly**: Positive relations, likely to trade
- **Neutral**: No strong feelings
- **Rival**: Competing interests, tense
- **Enemy**: Active or likely conflict

### Alliance Blocs (Likely to Form)

**Democratic Alliance**:
- Free City (leader)
- Vault Collective
- Old Guard

**Merchant Coalition**:
- Rust Brothers (leader)
- Corporate Remnant
- Sometimes Vault Collective

**Raider Conflict**:
- Crimson Horde vs. everyone (but especially Free City, Old Guard, Green Faith)

**Ideological Isolates**:
- Children of Atom (usually alone)
- Green Faith (peaceful isolationism)

## Faction Victory Preferences

Each faction has weighted preferences for victory conditions:

1. **Vault Collective**: Science 60%, Diplomatic 30%, Cultural 10%
2. **Rust Brothers**: Diplomatic 50%, Territorial 30%, Exodus 20%
3. **Green Faith**: Cultural 70%, Territorial 30%
4. **Corporate Remnant**: Diplomatic 50%, Territorial 40%, Exodus 10%
5. **Free City**: Cultural 50%, Diplomatic 40%, Territorial 10%
6. **Crimson Horde**: Raider King 60%, Territorial 40%
7. **Old Guard**: Diplomatic 50%, Territorial 50%
8. **Children of Atom**: Cultural 60%, Territorial 30%, Science 10%

AI will adapt strategy based on game state, but these are baseline preferences.
